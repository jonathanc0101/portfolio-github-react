About War

We can't kill each other legally without repercussions, unless we're at war

-Probably me

Conflict

War supposes that there is atleast one conflict between two groups of people, a conflict big enough that it's justified to make the other group submit to someone's will and even kill if necessary. Just to make a transfer of power, or to maybe change the course of history.

Rules

There is no game without rules, Jus in bello or also called right in war defines which people can be killed and the amount of force allowed. For example: You can't kill a medic or a civilian, you can't use mustard gas or nerve agent, etc.

There are rules that apply to everyone involved, but the people on the battlefield are not the players, they are merely tools. The big players are probably safe at home. This is not imperial rome anymore, politicians are not at the front, and the tools are not being used to build a brighter future.

To someone at war

Maybe that's true, maybe that's all true, but they came from a woman's womb, they need the same food as you to live, they feel love in the same way and their conviction is probably as strong as yours, or maybe they don't know why they're there, just wanted a little spice in their lives.

Whathever the personal reason, they came to this world alone, and will leave alone, just like you, they and you are merely instruments of someone else that is pulling the strings for some reason.

About Power

Meaning

Power can mean a lot of things and throughout history has changed it's meaning, from

  • Manpower: The amount of troops loyal to someone.

  • Political influence: The power to pass laws or convince politicians to make decisions.

  • International power: The power of a nation to influence others.

  • Economic power: The power of an entity to influence the economy.

Media

But there is another power that we normally don't take into account, it's the power that mass media have on us, on our way of thinking.

Evidence for this is the huge role that media outlets play on political elections, the effect of Twitter on the stock market, etc.

This power has not come alone by itself, we have succumbed to it, we have let ourselves be the product, giving cellphones to our kids since an early age, installing home banking apps to let anyone with our smartphone get access to our bank acount, shopping online what the site recommends us, watching what the algorithm feeds us, we have become sheep once again.

About Language

We all have a notion of what a language is, if you need to tell someone something you're using a language, we probably know that javascript is a language, English is a language, but they're very different. You can go to the shop and use one of this languages to buy a few apples, while, on the other hand, you can use the other one to make a webpage.

Type of language

What sets them appart is the type of language, broadly speaking there are two relevant types of language that we will discuss in here

  • Natural languages
  • Formal languages

In both of these languages there are rules, called grammar, and structure, also called syntax.

Every language has the basic notion these two things

  • Grammar

    This defines how you form "words" in english, instructions in ASSEMBLER, etc.

  • Syntax

    This defines how you form sentences, it's not the same to say "hello sir I want to pet your little dog called sausage" instead of "hello dog I want to pet your little sir, sausage called", I added a comma there but you probably get how this works.

Programming and formal languages

So, having said this we can continue with the big topic, programming and formal languages.

In formal languages whatever you say has only one interpretation, no ambiguity on the effect of the symbols and words you say. There is no equivalent to "I like you" in a formal language, because "I like you" can be interpreted in a few different ways depending on the context.

This is useful because if we want something done exactly as we say but we don't want to do it ourselves we need a system to communicate exactly what we want done. (you might think that everyone who uses a computer is treating it as a slave, and you're probably right)

So ok, we will use a formal language made up to communicate with a computer, also called a programming language, but there is a problem, we can't ask a computer directly with English or any other language that we know, but don't worry, that's already been gradually solved by some really clever people since computers are invented so you can go and write some easy to read Python code and your computer will run it and do what it says.

What is this magic?

Well, in hell, or also called the 70's and 80's, you would code in assembler, or as close to zeroes and ones as you can get without coding in zeroes and ones. Because that was all that a computer understood. But then some really smart people came up with the idea of a language that you can translate to assembler, but it wasn't as horrible to write, then someone else iterated that process with the higher level language(binary is the lowest, we can only go up), it was like making a tool to make a sharper tool, and bam! 40 years later we probably won't have to code in assembler unless, unless we're making an Operative System or something like that.

This process of translation usually goes two ways

  • Compilation

    It occurs once over the source code and the computer understads a binary file called program object.

  • Interpretation

    It occurs every time we want to run the code, but it gives us the advantage of being probably multi-platform.
