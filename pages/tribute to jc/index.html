Julius Caesar

A great grey figure in history

The first emperor

He may be wrongly considered the first emperor of Rome, but he certainly did occupy a place similar to that of an emperor, near the end of his ruling he

  • Dressed in purple (like the kings of Rome did).
  • Ordered to put his statue near the ones of the seven kings of Rome suggesting that he was also one of them.
  • Made the senate name him dictator for life, accounting for the fact that dictatorship was a position of power in the (at the time) Roman republic, that normally lasted 6 months.

Importance of the facts in the context

You might say "why does all this matter? I get it, dictator sounds bad". But there is more to this than that. Dictatorship was a position of power granted to someone to be "in charge" for a period of six months originally, albeit turbulent times had to endanger the republic for this title to be given; To imagine someone to be able to pass laws without consulting the senate, to have power over a big portion of the army, it's unthinkable nowadays. Imagine that the president wanted to make everyone stay home for ten consecutive months consecuently stopping the economy. (Ups, that happened in Argentina in 2021)
